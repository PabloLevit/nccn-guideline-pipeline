Q 5
--------------------
Yes, there is a recommendation for MRD (minimal residual disease).

1. Detectable MRD after achieving remission is associated with a higher risk of relapse in both children and adults with AML.
2. The recommended methods for MRD assessment include quantitative molecular assays like real-time quantitative  () and multicolor  cytometry.
3. MRD monitoring strategies may include testing after achieving morphologic remission, following allogeneic transplantation, and at specified time points based on treatment regimens.
4. If MRD is persistently positive, consideration should be given to clinical trials, alternative therapies, or allogeneic HCT.
