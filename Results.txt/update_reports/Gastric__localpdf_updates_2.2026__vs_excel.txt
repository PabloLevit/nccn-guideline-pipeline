1.3 — Is there a recommendation for PDL1 / MSI / TMB test?
STATUS: REVIEW
##########################################################################################
EXCEL (existing answer lines):
- PDL1 - PD-L1 testing is universally recommended at diagnosis for all gastric cancer patients who may receive PD-1/PD-L1 inhibitors and must be performed by IHC on FFPE tumor tissue in CLIA-approved laboratories using an approved companion diagnostic.
- MSI - Universal testing for MSI or MMR deficiency is recommended for all newly diagnosed gastric cancer patients using PCR/NGS or IHC on FFPE tissue in CLIA-approved laboratories, with results reported as MSI-H or dMMR per CAP guidelines, enabling genetic counseling referral when appropriate and, when using NGS, allowing simultaneous assessment of MSI alongside other relevant molecular alterations.
- TMB - TMB assessment may be performed as part of NGS testing in CLIA-approved laboratories, which can be considered later in the clinical course for patients with sufficient tumor tissue, allowing simultaneous evaluation of TMB alongside other genomic alterations such as MSI, amplifications, and deletions.

2. — Is there a recommendation for ctDNA NGS?
STATUS: REVIEW
##########################################################################################
EXCEL (existing answer lines):
- YES
- Circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) analysis from blood can identify genomic alterations, including mutations and gene fusions, in gastric cancer and may help detect targetable changes or evolving resistant clones
- Multigene panel testing using ctDNA may be considered in CLIA-approved laboratories when tumor tissue is limited or biopsy is not feasible in advanced or metastatic disease, and negative results should be interpreted cautiously, as they do not exclude the presence of tumor.
- Liquid biopsy using circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) allows detection of genomic alterations and gene fusions in gastric cancer from blood, helping identify targetable mutations and tumor clonal evolution associated with treatment response or resistance.
